 Here by the overline on negative examples $\overline{E^{-}}$ we denote the set of the negated sentences of $E^{-}$. When specifying the consistency condition for the negative examples by $B \cup H \not\models E^{-}$ we mean that no example $e^{-} \in E^{-}$ should be implied by $B \cup H$, therefore $E^{-}$ is meant to be a disjunction (rather than a conjunction like $E^{+}$) of examples. Therefore, $\overline{E^{-}} \equiv \neg E^{-}$.
